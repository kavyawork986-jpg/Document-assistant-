# Zoho Corporation

## Company Overview
Zoho Corporation is an Indian multinational technology company headquartered in Chennai, India (with a global base), known for its broad suite of cloud-based business software (CRM, productivity, finance, and more). Founded in 1996 by Sridhar Vembu and Tony Thomas, Zoho is famously bootstrapped, privately held, with no outside investors or debt.

## Mission & Values
Zoho's guiding philosophy is "freedom" and long-term, R&D-driven growth independent of external capital. Rather than formally codifying values, the company transmits its philosophy through leadership example: privacy-respecting software, customer focus, frugality, self-reliance, and giving opportunity to people from non-elite and rural backgrounds. Zoho publicly commits to never selling customer data or showing third-party ads.

## Code of Conduct / Ethics
Zoho emphasises strong data-privacy and ethical-business practices as a core differentiator, stating it does not sell customer data or rely on ad-based surveillance models. As a private company it follows internal codes governing integrity, privacy, and fair dealing rather than publishing extensive listed-company governance documents.

## Culture & Work Model
Zoho is known for an unconventional culture: it builds software with small teams in informal settings and has deliberately located development campuses in rural villages such as Tenkasi, rather than only in metros. Founder Sridhar Vembu lives a minimalist lifestyle and emphasises community, education, and proximity to nature. The company favours long-term thinking over short-term growth metrics.

## Diversity, Equity & Inclusion
Zoho's most distinctive inclusion effort is socioeconomic: through Zoho Schools of Learning (formerly Zoho University), it trains students—including those from rural and non-degree backgrounds—and hires them directly, with a meaningful share of its workforce coming from this program. This widens access to tech careers beyond traditional engineering-college pipelines.

## CSR / Sustainability (ESG)
Zoho invests in rural development, education, and local economic upliftment, consistent with its rural-campus strategy. The founder's emphasis on rural revival, farming, and community schooling is central to the company's social philosophy. Zoho generally avoids the formal ESG-report format of listed firms, reflecting its private, bootstrapped status.

## Careers & Benefits (as publicly stated)
Zoho hires both experienced professionals and students through Zoho Schools of Learning, offering an alternative path into tech without a conventional degree. It provides on-the-job training and a campus-style work environment as publicly described. Specific benefit details are not extensively published given its private status.
