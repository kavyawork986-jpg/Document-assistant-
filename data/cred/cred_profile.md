# CRED (Dreamplug Technologies)

## Company Overview
CRED, operated by Dreamplug Technologies, is an Indian fintech company headquartered in Bengaluru, India. Founded in 2018 by Kunal Shah (who earlier founded Freecharge), CRED began as a members-only app that rewards users for paying credit-card bills on time and expanded into payments, credit, and commerce for a financially responsible, premium user base.

## Mission & Values
CRED was built on the philosophy of rewarding trustworthy, financially responsible individuals and improving financial behaviour. It is known for a design-first, premium brand experience and a reward-driven ecosystem. The company emphasises trust, design excellence, and elevating the credit experience in India.

## Code of Conduct / Ethics
CRED maintains internal codes governing integrity, anti-bribery and anti-corruption, conflicts of interest, fair dealing, data privacy, and information security, with channels to report concerns. As a fintech handling sensitive financial data, security and compliance are emphasised.

## Culture & Work Model
CRED's culture is characterised by experimentation, high ownership, fast execution, and a strong emphasis on design and craft. Founder Kunal Shah is known for his views on trust, incentives, and consumer behaviour, which shape the company's product philosophy. Work arrangements are largely office-centric for many roles.

## Diversity, Equity & Inclusion
CRED runs inclusion and well-being initiatives across gender and background and emphasises a high-ownership, merit-driven environment. It reports people initiatives as a private company.

## CSR / Sustainability (ESG)
As a private company, CRED follows responsible-business practices and participates in ecosystem and community initiatives rather than publishing the formal ESG-report format of large listed firms. Its core consumer focus is encouraging responsible financial behaviour.

## Careers & Benefits (as publicly stated)
CRED hires across engineering, design, product, and business roles and offers stock options (ESOPs) for eligible roles. It highlights well-being and design-led culture benefits on its careers communications. Specific benefits vary by role.
