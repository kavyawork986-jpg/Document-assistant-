# Infosys

## Company Overview
Infosys is an Indian multinational IT and consulting company headquartered in Bengaluru, India. Founded in 1981 by N. R. Narayana Murthy and six co-founders, it became one of India's most influential technology companies and a pioneer of the global delivery model. Infosys provides digital services, consulting, application development, cloud, and AI-led transformation to clients worldwide.

## Mission & Values
Infosys's core values are captured in the acronym C-LIFE: Client Value, Leadership by Example, Integrity and Transparency, Fairness, and Excellence. These values form the basis of its Code of Conduct and guide day-to-day decisions. Infosys is historically associated with strong corporate governance and a "compassionate capitalism" philosophy championed by its founders.

## Code of Conduct / Ethics
The Infosys Code of Conduct and Ethics sets out the company's core values, shared responsibilities, and commitments, providing guidance on integrity, transparency, anti-corruption, conflicts of interest, insider-trading prevention, and protection of confidential information. The company is known for high standards of disclosure and an independent, well-regarded board.

## Culture & Work Model
Infosys is known for its emphasis on training and a meritocratic, process-driven culture. Its Mysuru campus houses one of the world's largest corporate training centres, where new hires undergo structured foundation programs. The company operates a hybrid work model and invests heavily in continuous learning through its internal platforms.

## Diversity, Equity & Inclusion
Infosys runs structured diversity programs with a focus on gender diversity, hiring of people with disabilities, and inclusion across nationalities and backgrounds. It publishes diversity metrics and has set goals to improve women's representation, supported by mentoring, return-to-work, and employee resource groups.

## CSR / Sustainability (ESG)
The Infosys Foundation supports education, healthcare, rural development, and the arts. Infosys has publicly announced that it achieved carbon neutrality and maintains commitments around renewable energy, water, and waste in its ESG reporting. Education and digital skilling (including programs like Infosys Springboard) are central to its community work.

## Careers & Benefits (as publicly stated)
Infosys recruits extensively from campuses and runs well-known early-career and reskilling programs. It offers structured learning and certification, health and insurance coverage, and well-being support as publicly described on its careers and ESG pages. Specific benefits vary by geography and role.
