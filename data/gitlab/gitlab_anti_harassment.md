# GitLab Anti-Harassment Policy

## Overview

GitLab is committed to maintaining a safe, respectful, and inclusive workplace for all team members globally. Everyone at GitLab has a responsibility to prevent and stop harassment.

This policy applies to all team members — employees and contractors — across all locations. It covers interactions with colleagues, customers, vendors, and community members across all communication formats including video calls, email, documents, chat, and in-person meetings.

## Prohibited Conduct

### 1. Sexual Harassment
Sexual harassment includes:
- Unwelcome sexual advances or requests for sexual favors
- Offensive comments of a sexual nature
- Unwanted touching or physical contact
- Deliberate misgendering or deadnaming
- Sharing explicit or suggestive content in the workplace

### 2. Discrimination
Discrimination based on any legally protected characteristic is prohibited, including:
- Race, color, ethnicity, national origin
- Religion or belief
- Sex, gender identity, gender expression
- Age
- Disability
- Sexual orientation
- Any other legally protected status in applicable jurisdictions

### 3. Bullying and Workplace Violence
- Threatening or violent behavior of any kind
- Coercive or intimidating behavior
- Repeated demeaning treatment of a team member
- Actions designed to undermine, isolate, or humiliate

### 4. Retaliation
Any adverse action taken against a team member for reporting harassment, participating in an investigation, or opposing discriminatory practices is strictly prohibited and will itself be treated as a serious violation.

## Reporting

Team members may report incidents to:
- Chief People Officer
- Team Member Relations (teammemberrelations@gitlab.com)
- People Business Partners
- EthicsPoint (anonymous ethics hotline)
- Lighthouse Services: 833-480-0010 (English, US/Canada)

GitLab encourages addressing issues directly when it is safe to do so. Formal investigation procedures are available when direct resolution is not appropriate or possible.

## Disciplinary Framework

Consequences are graduated based on severity:
- **Level 1**: Suspension and formal apology
- **Level 2**: Final written warning
- **Level 3**: Termination
- **Level 4**: Termination and referral to law enforcement for criminal offenses

## Regional Variations

The policy includes country and state-specific procedures for jurisdictions including:
- Netherlands
- Belgium
- Japan
- India
- United Kingdom
- Ireland
- Multiple U.S. states

Each regional variation reflects local legal requirements while maintaining GitLab's zero-tolerance stance on harassment and discrimination.
