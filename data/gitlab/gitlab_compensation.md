# GitLab Compensation

## Philosophy

GitLab's compensation philosophy is to attract and reward team members who deliver meaningful results through collaboration while operating with efficiency. Compensation is designed to be competitive, transparent, and fair across a globally distributed workforce.

## Components of Compensation

### Base Salary
Annual guaranteed pay determined by role, level, and geographic location. GitLab targets the 50th percentile of software industry compensation for base salaries. Ranges are built from local market data using Radford benchmarks.

### Variable Pay
- **Director Bonuses** — Paid semi-annually based on company performance
- **Sales Commissions** — Structured as On-Target Earnings (OTE), combining base salary with commission targets for sales roles

### Equity
Restricted Stock Units (RSUs) that vest over time. Equity is granted:
- At hire (initial grant)
- Through annual refresh grants
- Upon promotion

Equity aligns team members with GitLab's long-term success and is a meaningful part of total compensation.

## Compensation Timing

Adjustments to compensation occur at defined points:
- **Hire** — Initial package based on market range, location, and experience
- **Annual Compensation Review (ACR)** — Effective May 1 each year, addressing merit increases and market alignment
- **Promotions** — Processed twice yearly: May 1 and November 1
- **Internal Moves** — Handled outside regular cycles as needed

## Job Architecture

GitLab uses a standardized Job Architecture framework with defined job families, levels, and career tracks. Compensation ranges span typically from -20% to +20% around the market midpoint for each role and level.

**Compa-ratio** is the measure of how an individual's salary compares to the midpoint of their range. It provides transparency about where someone sits relative to the market.

As of May 1, 2026, all team members can view their compensation ranges in Workday.

## Transparency

GitLab is committed to pay transparency. Team members have access to the compensation ranges for their roles and can understand how their pay is determined. This transparency is consistent with GitLab's broader value of making information available by default.
