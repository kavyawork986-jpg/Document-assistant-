# GitLab Values

GitLab's organizational culture is built on **CREDIT** — six interconnected core values that guide decision-making and behavior.

## The Six Values

### 1. Collaboration
Team members work together effectively, helping others even when not directly related to immediate goals. This includes kindness, transparency, and assuming positive intent.

### 2. Results for Customers
Everything serves making customers successful. This value sits at the hierarchy's top, emphasizing ambitious goals, understanding customer needs, and measuring impact rather than activity.

### 3. Efficiency
Producing results without wasting time, materials, or energy. Prioritizes global optimization over local convenience and emphasizes documentation and boring solutions.

### 4. Diversity, Inclusion & Belonging
Creating an environment where people from every background thrive. Includes asynchronous communication, embracing uncomfortable conversations, and valuing neurodiversity.

### 5. Iteration
Breaking work into small chunks to get fast feedback and reach desired outcomes efficiently. Requires clear vision but permits minimal viable changes.

### 6. Transparency
Making information public by default to reduce contribution barriers and improve collaboration. Being direct and articulating reasoning behind decisions.

## Key Principles

These values are **actionable**, not aspirational. They prevent the "five dysfunctions" of teams and enable distributed decision-making across the organization. The handbook emphasizes that operating principles clarify what each value means specifically at GitLab, distinguishing their interpretation from other companies' approaches.

## Collaboration

Collaboration means helping others across the organization, not just within your immediate team. Key behaviors include:
- Kindness and assuming positive intent in all interactions
- Transparency in communication and decision-making
- Working across functions and time zones effectively
- Sharing information openly rather than hoarding it

## Results for Customers

Results means achieving meaningful outcomes for customers, not just completing tasks. This includes:
- Setting ambitious, measurable goals
- Understanding what customers actually need
- Measuring impact rather than activity
- Holding yourself accountable to outcomes

## Efficiency

Efficiency means being effective without unnecessary waste. Principles include:
- Prefer boring, proven solutions over novel ones
- Document processes so others don't repeat work
- Optimize for the whole organization, not just your team
- Avoid unnecessary meetings and synchronous communication

## Diversity, Inclusion & Belonging

Everyone should feel welcome and able to contribute. This means:
- Actively including people from all backgrounds
- Using asynchronous communication to include all time zones
- Embracing and learning from uncomfortable conversations
- Valuing different ways of thinking, including neurodiversity

## Iteration

Ship the smallest thing that delivers value, then improve. This means:
- Break large projects into small, releasable pieces
- Get feedback early and often
- Accept that the first version won't be perfect
- Move forward rather than waiting for the ideal solution

## Transparency

Default to making information public. This means:
- Share decisions and their reasoning openly
- Make the handbook and processes publicly accessible
- Be direct and honest rather than diplomatic to the point of being unclear
- Transparency enables everyone to contribute and hold each other accountable
