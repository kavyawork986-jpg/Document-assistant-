# GitLab Hiring and Talent Acquisition

## Mission

Through exceptional partnership and experiences, we attract and hire results-driven talent to build high-performing distributed teams that accelerate GitLab's growth.

## Core Principles

### Experience
The Talent Acquisition team functions as a partner and advisor — not just a recruiter. Their role is to help build strong teams and contribute to organizational culture. Every candidate interaction is a representation of GitLab's values.

### Inclusivity
Fair and equitable hiring processes are built into every stage of recruitment. GitLab actively works to shape diverse teams and remove bias from how candidates are evaluated and selected.

### Predictability
Data-driven decision-making enables accurate forecasting and right-time hiring. The team uses metrics to ensure they are hiring at the pace the business needs, without over or under-hiring.

## Resources by Role

### For Candidates
GitLab provides a dedicated handbook page explaining the recruitment process and a privacy policy outlining how candidate data is handled. GitLab is committed to transparency — candidates should know what to expect at every stage.

### For Interviewers and Hiring Managers
Training courses and preparatory materials are available to ensure consistent and effective hiring practices across the organization. All interviewers are expected to complete interview training before conducting interviews.

### For Recruiting Staff
Dedicated playbooks and strategies are available for recruiters and candidate experience specialists to ensure a high-quality, consistent process.

## Key Programs

- **Referral Program** — Team members can refer candidates and are recognized for successful hires
- **Talent Brand** — GitLab actively builds its employer brand to attract aligned candidates
- **Background Checks** — Conducted as part of the standard hiring process
- **Visa Sponsorship** — Coordinated through the People team where applicable

## Hiring Philosophy

GitLab hires for results, not credentials. The focus is on a candidate's ability to deliver outcomes aligned with GitLab's values and mission, not on where they went to school or what titles they held. GitLab values demonstrated skills and judgment over pedigree.

Hiring decisions are made based on structured interviews and defined criteria established before the process begins — not on gut feeling or subjective impressions formed after the fact.
