# Zomato (Eternal)

## Company Overview
Zomato is an Indian food-delivery and restaurant-discovery company headquartered in Gurugram, India. Founded in 2008 by Deepinder Goyal and Pankaj Chaddah (originally as FoodieBay), it became one of India's largest food-tech platforms and listed publicly in 2021. Its parent entity was later renamed Eternal, housing brands such as Zomato, Blinkit (quick commerce), and Hyperpure.

## Mission & Values
Zomato's concise mission is "better food for more people," extending to a broader vision that includes making India malnutrition-free. Founder Deepinder Goyal has said the goal is "not to be the best company in the world, but the best company for the world." Its stated cultural values include customer obsession, bias for action, respect for merchants, operational excellence, and integrity and transparency, alongside traits like ownership, humility, and judgment.

## Code of Conduct / Ethics
As a listed company, Zomato/Eternal maintains a Code of Conduct covering integrity, anti-bribery and anti-corruption, conflicts of interest, fair dealing with restaurant and delivery partners, data privacy, anti-harassment, and insider-trading prevention, with whistle-blower mechanisms.

## Culture & Work Model
Zomato is known for a transparent, founder-led, fast-moving culture and candid public communication. It has experimented with progressive people policies and emphasises ownership and customer/merchant focus. The company supports flexible work arrangements for many roles.

## Diversity, Equity & Inclusion
Zomato has publicly introduced inclusive people policies, including period leave and gender-neutral and extended parental-leave approaches communicated publicly, and supports inclusion of delivery partners. It promotes diversity across gender and background and reports related efforts.

## CSR / Sustainability (ESG)
Zomato runs the "Feeding India" initiative addressing hunger and food access, and has public commitments around climate (including efforts toward greener deliveries and electric vehicles), packaging, and reducing its environmental footprint, reported in its ESG disclosures.

## Careers & Benefits (as publicly stated)
Zomato hires across technology, operations, and corporate roles. It offers stock options for eligible roles and has publicly highlighted progressive leave policies (such as period leave and parental-leave provisions) on its blog and careers communications. Specific benefits vary by role.
